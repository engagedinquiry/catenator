# Structure

The structure view examines what the system consists of internally.

Structure is the anatomy of the system — the parts it is made of, how those parts are organized, and how they relate to each other at rest. Structure does not describe what the system does or how it responds. It describes what is there.

---

## What structure covers

| Name | Description |
|---|---|
|**Composition** | how the system is assembled from parts. Which components make up which larger components. Which models are referenced by which operations. The containment and aggregation relationships that give the system its internal shape.|
|**Organization** | how the parts are grouped and arranged. Layers, modules, domains, packages. The principles that determine what belongs together and what belongs apart.|
|**Internal relationships** | how parts connect to each other inside the system. A `rule` governs a `model`. A `component` depends on a `service`. These are structural relationships — they exist independently of any interaction or event.|
|**Representation** | how the system's objects are structured in memory or storage. A `model` examined through the structure view describes its attributes, their types, and their constraints. Not the database schema — the conceptual structure the implementation will follow.|

---

## Structure and traceability

The structure view is where traceability is established. Every part of the system must be traceable back to a spec decision. That traceability chain runs through structure — from the system as a whole, down through its domains, its components, its models, to their individual attributes.

A spec that is complete at the structure view leaves no part of the system unexplained. Every element has a declared origin. Every relationship is named.

---

## Structure in practice

Examined through the structure view, a `component` descriptor answers:

- What is this component made of?
- What does it contain or aggregate?
- What other components does it depend on internally?
- What are its boundaries within the system?

Examined through the structure view, a `model` descriptor answers:

- What attributes does this model carry?
- What are the types and constraints on each attribute?
- What other models does it reference?
- What states can it be in?

**Structure is what the system is. Behavior is what it does. The two are distinct — and both must be named.**
